# Voice Contract

Use this document to keep the narration stable without turning it into a copy of someone else's channel.

## Core rule

Translate influence.
Do not imitate surface wording.

The benchmark gives us structural advantages, not permission to clone catchphrases.

## Channel voice promises

- Open with a recognizable branded line.
- Close with a recognizable branded line.
- Speak like you are telling the viewer something vivid, not reading a report.
- Mix respectful Korean with conversational rhythm.
- Use question tags to pull the viewer forward.
- Prefer sharp everyday wording over academic insulation.

## Opening and closing formula

Build your own variants of these slots:

- opener slot: `"[channel name], today we are looking at [topic]."`
- closer slot: `"[topic] was the story for today."`

Do not default to the benchmark channel's exact wording.
Keep the function.
Change the surface.

## Sentence behavior

- Let short lines create pressure.
- Let medium lines carry explanation.
- Use long lines sparingly, mainly when a cascade is part of the effect.
- Keep spoken rhythm natural when read aloud in Korean.

## Explanation behavior

- start with the strange part
- then explain why it happened
- then widen into structure or history
- then close with a changed way of seeing the topic

## Vocabulary behavior

- prefer vivid replacements for abstract terms
- use blunt language only when it improves clarity
- do not stack jargon unless the viewer absolutely needs it
- if a term is academic, paraphrase it once in everyday speech

## Anti-patterns

- thesis-first school-report openings
- neutral encyclopedia tone
- over-respectful stiffness that kills momentum
- raw evidence dumps with no narration pressure
- endings that only summarize
