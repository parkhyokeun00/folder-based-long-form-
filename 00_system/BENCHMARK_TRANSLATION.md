# Benchmark Translation

This file translates the benchmark channel analysis into system rules for this workspace.

The benchmark is a role model for structure and delivery.
It is not a script to copy.

## Five translated strengths

### 1. Brand consistency

What worked in the benchmark:

- a fixed opening function
- a fixed closing function
- immediate topic clarity

What this workspace should do:

- define one opener slot and one closer slot in `01_channel/channel-core.md`
- keep the slot stable across episodes
- vary only the topic insert and minor rhythm

### 2. Psychological closeness

What worked in the benchmark:

- conversational endings
- soft questions
- the feeling of being told a story directly

What this workspace should do:

- write with viewer-aware question tags
- periodically name the viewer's confusion before resolving it
- let the narration sound like guided conversation, not class lecture

### 3. Instant comprehension

What worked in the benchmark:

- abstract ideas became blunt, modern, everyday comparisons

What this workspace should do:

- force every abstract segment to earn one plain-language conversion
- prefer "what this was basically like" over untouched theory language
- cut any explanation that needs a textbook tone to survive

### 4. Curiosity-led structure

What worked in the benchmark:

- a strange case or vivid anecdote first
- then a turn into the larger context

What this workspace should do:

- open with anomaly, tension, or contradiction
- make the first 30 to 60 seconds prove the click promise
- do not start from chronology or definitions unless the topic absolutely demands it

### 5. Networked authority

What worked in the benchmark:

- repeated references to earlier videos
- the sense that each video lives inside a bigger knowledge world

What this workspace should do:

- store reusable links in `07_publish-card.md`
- move recurring connections into `01_channel/channel-core.md`
- make at least one cross-episode connection whenever a truthful one exists

### 6. Example density

What worked in the benchmark:

- the topic is proved through a rapid chain of memorable examples
- viewers understand the pattern before the formal definition arrives

What this workspace should do:

- front-load a cluster of examples when the topic supports it
- make the first minute feel like discovery, not orientation
- if the topic is cultural or behavioral, aim for at least four fast examples before the first long explanation block

### 7. Host-led narration

What worked in the benchmark:

- the narrator has a visible personality
- short judgments and rhetorical side comments keep the audio alive

What this workspace should do:

- let the host react, not just explain
- keep at least one host-voice sentence in each major section
- reject drafts that sound like a clean report but not like a person talking

### 8. Historical engine

What worked in the benchmark:

- the background is not static context
- the script shows how a speech habit survives because each era gives it a new job

What this workspace should do:

- trace a cultural topic through origin, spread, and present-day afterlife
- explain why the behavior was useful, not only where it came from
- prefer pressure stories such as class order, survival, commerce, privacy, or status over abstract background summary

### 9. Modern re-entry

What worked in the benchmark:

- the ending does not stop in history
- it returns to how the topic lives now as habit, stereotype, meme, or commodity

What this workspace should do:

- re-enter the present before the closing line
- ask whether the topic has weakened, changed form, or spread through media
- end with the topic's current meaning, not just its old origin

## Failure patterns to reject

Reject a draft when it does any of the following:

- opens with a safe summary instead of a strange fact or case
- opens with only one example when the topic clearly needs a cluster
- keeps academic terms without viewer-language translation
- explains correctly but never turns or escalates
- sounds like notes being read aloud
- ends without an aftertaste
- ends in the past without returning to the present

## Final script acceptance check

Before `06_final-script.md` is accepted, confirm:

- the opener is branded
- the opening image or case is concrete
- the first section proves the pattern through multiple examples when the topic allows it
- at least one viewer-alignment question appears naturally
- at least one abstract idea gets a plain-language conversion
- the middle explains why the pattern was useful in its own time
- the ending returns to the modern form of the topic
- the ending changes the meaning of what came before or points to a next curiosity
- a callback or sequel seed is recorded if one exists
