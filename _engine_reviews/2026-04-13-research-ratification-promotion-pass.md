## What looked genuinely reusable

- the result quality came from separating `자료 조사` from narration instead of blending them into one step
- the research note preserved both adopted claims and rejected claims, which prevented a vivid but weak myth-chain from re-entering the script
- the strongest stability move was not `more sources` in the abstract, but a visible source ladder plus a visible claim ladder
- the draft stayed trustworthy because chronology, scope, and mechanism were tested before the causal story was narrated
- helper research widened the search, but the final spine still depended on main-agent verification

## Accepted promotions

### 1. Research-note-first ratification

If a topic depends on disputed history, science, policy, or a viral claim, the system should create or reopen a research note before the outline hardens.

### 2. Source ladder before confidence

Narration confidence should follow source authority, not repetition count.

### 3. Adopted-versus-rejected claim tracking

Strong research notes should preserve what was excluded, not only what was kept.

### 4. Myth-chain testing

When a topic arrives as a clean domino chain, test chronology, scope, mechanism, and representative evidence before the script repeats it.

### 5. Main-agent final verification

Parallel research assistance is useful, but final script-safe claims should still be ratified by the master agent.

## Rejected or local-only lessons

- requiring exactly two research helpers is local to that run, not engine law
- the top-level `0000_` session record is useful, but not every run needs that exact filename or root placement
- the no-summary ending choice was partially user-specific and should not be elevated into a universal research rule
