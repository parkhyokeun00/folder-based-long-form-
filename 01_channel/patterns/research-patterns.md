# Research Patterns

This page stores reusable evidence and ratification patterns that should survive beyond any single topic.

## Pattern 1. Research note first for disputed claims

- if the topic depends on a historical myth, scientific controversy, policy dispute, or viral one-line story, create or reopen a research note before the outline hardens
- script-safe material should be selected from that note, not reconstructed from memory after browsing

## Pattern 2. Source ladder before confidence

- rank sources by authority before turning them into narration
- if the strongest support is secondary or tertiary commentary, either keep the wording cautious or keep researching
- one authoritative contradiction can outweigh several weak repetitions

## Pattern 3. Claim ladder, not one blob

- separate verified fact, supported interpretation, working hypothesis, and rejected claim
- keep tempting but weak claims in a rejected or downgraded section so they stop sneaking back into the draft

## Pattern 4. Test the clean chain

- when a viral claim arrives as `A -> B -> C`, test scope, chronology, mechanism, and representative evidence
- if one link fails, narrate the complexity instead of repeating the meme

## Pattern 5. Main-agent ratification

- parallel researchers can widen the search, but the master agent should independently verify the high-impact lines that enter the script spine
- do not launder borrowed certainty through confident host tone

## Pattern 6. Exclusion is part of the research

- strong research notes explain what was left out and why
- a narrower but source-safe spine is stronger than a dramatic but weak one
